2. Brief on Experiment & Methodology 
 * Objective: To validate a newly developed "Selective Attention Game" by comparing it against a standard "Lab Task" (gold standard). 
 * Design: A 2 × 2 Mixed Factorial Design. 
   * Factor 1 (Between-Subjects): Target Load (Single Target vs. Multiple Target). Participants were randomly assigned to one of these two groups. 
   * Factor 2 (Within-Subjects): Modality (Game vs. Lab). Every participant completed the task in both modalities. 
 * Procedure: 
   * Group A (N=21): Performed the Single Target Game AND Single Target Lab Task. 
   * Group B (N=16): Performed the Multi-Target Game AND Multi-Target Lab Task. 
   * Counterbalancing: Half played game first, half did lab tasks first.

This project is based on a selective attention task, where participants have to quickly identify targets among distractors. The difficulty of this task depends on how many targets they need to focus on.

For our dataset, we had 37 participants, divided into two groups based on target load :

One group performed a single-target task, which is easier.
The other performed a multiple-target task, which increases cognitive load and difficulty.

Now, for both these groups, we introduced two different modalities:

A lab-based task on a desktop (traditional method)
A game-based task on a smartphone (gamified version)

So essentially, every participant performed their task in both lab and game settings.

Based on this setup, we focused on four main research questions:

RQ1: Whether game performance correlates with lab performance (validity)
RQ2: Whether multiple-target tasks are harder than single-target
RQ3: Whether the game changes performance compared to the lab
RQ4: How performance changes across different levels in the game

And finally, we also place special focus on the learning effect, to see whether participants improve or show fatigue as they progress through the game levels.

That’s the overall idea of our study

Dataset explaination:
The Participants
Total Sample: There are 37 people participating in the study.

The "Between-Subjects" Factor (Target Load)
"Between-subjects" simply means that the participants are divided into completely separate groups. Each person only experiences one version of this specific challenge.

Group 1 (21 people): They are assigned to only look for a single target.

Group 2 (16 people): They are assigned to only look for multiple targets.

Why it's called this: The comparison happens between different subjects (people). A person from Group 1 never plays the Group 2 version, and vice versa.

The "Within-Subjects" Factor (Modality)
"Within-subjects" means that every single participant gets to experience all versions of this specific condition, regardless of which group they are in.

Condition 1: The laboratory task (playing on a desktop computer).

Condition 2: The gamified task (playing on a smartphone).

Why it's called this: The comparison happens within the same subject. Every single one of the 37 people plays on both the computer and the phone.
